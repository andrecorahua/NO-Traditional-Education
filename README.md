### No Traditional Education: Economic Analysis for Tech Founders
This repository contains a series of research studies and data analyses focused on evaluating the economic viability and opportunity cost of the traditional education system in light of the demands of high-growth technology startups (Deep Tech) in the Peruvian and Latin American context.

#### Tesis Central
Through a rigorous analysis of financial indicators, return on investment (ROI), and actual graduation times, this study concludes that the traditional university system represents an inefficient allocation of capital and time for tech entrepreneurs.

For a Deep Tech founder, where rapid iteration and early market entry are critical, the current academic model presents significant barriers:

Declining ROI: The wage premium for each year of college education has fallen steadily over the past two decades, with nearly 40% of degree programs showing a negative net return.
“Overeducation” Phenomenon: One-third of graduates end up in jobs that do not require their level of education, highlighting a mismatch between academic offerings and the actual needs of the tech industry.
Massive Opportunity Cost: The actual time to graduation (which typically ranges from 5 to 8 years) represents a loss of income and market experience exceeding S/ 290,000—capital that could be directly invested in technology development and product-market fit.
Rigidity vs. Agility: While the traditional system prioritizes static theory, the Deep Tech ecosystem demands integrative, high-risk learning that can only be acquired through hands-on execution and the use of alternative learning pathways (bootcamps, specialized certifications, and mentoring networks).
